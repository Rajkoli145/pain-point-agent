## Patterns & Triggers
People are frustrated with AI when they feel it's replacing human thinking, making coding easier but less fulfilling, or creating security risks. They are trying to automate tasks, build successful side projects, or navigate the tech industry, but are met with frustration and disappointment. They have tried using AI tools, attending startup events, and seeking advice from others, but these solutions often fail to address their concerns.

## Objections & Blockers
People are stopped from solving these problems themselves due to a lack of understanding of AI, limited resources, or the complexity of the issues. They have tried using AI tools, but these often create more problems than they solve. They are also hesitant to seek help from others, fearing that their concerns will be dismissed or that they will be seen as unqualified.

The biggest opportunity is to create an AI-ethics tool that addresses the frustration and concern surrounding AI.
