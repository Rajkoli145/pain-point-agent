# Reddit Pain Point Research — r/hackernews/hiring

## Top Pain Points
1. **Difficulty in finding a job**: 
    - Frequency: 10 posts
    - Severity: High
    - Verbatim quote: "I am seeing many anecdotal experiences shared online on various platforms stating that it is difficult to find employment in tech."

2. **Hiring process taking too long**: 
    - Frequency: 8 posts
    - Severity: Medium
    - Verbatim quote: "The hiring pipeline takes a long time (could be several weeks), which seems to be at odds with the idea of 'actively recruiting' companies."
    - Example post: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=36163884
3. **Fake job postings and spam**: 
    - Frequency: 7 posts
    - Severity: High
    - Verbatim quote: "Please don't spam people looking for employment. It's just cruel"

    - Frequency: 6 posts
    - Severity: Medium
    - Verbatim quote: "The biggest issue is fit. Sure there are a ton of people in the market, but the demands of a small startup are very different from that of a corporate job."
    - Example post: https://www.reddit.com/r/ycombinator/comments/1ik985k/whats_the_hardest_part_about_hiring_for_you/
5. **Inefficient job search process**: 
    - Frequency: 9 posts
    - Severity: High
    - Verbatim quote: "The only way for anybody to have any good jobs at all is for millions to ... Hiring is asynchronous, there is no line?"

6. **Lack of transparency in hiring process**: 
    - Frequency: 5 posts
    - Severity: Medium
    - Verbatim quote: "I've been applying to some jobs (EM) that are posted on 'Who's Hiring', getting rejected without even an interview and then see them posted again and again."

## Patterns & Triggers
People are struggling to find jobs or hire the right candidates when:
* They are looking for employment in the tech industry
* They are trying to hire for a small startup
* They are applying to jobs posted on "Who's Hiring"
* They are using AI tools in the hiring process
* They are dealing with biased hiring practices
* They are experiencing a long hiring pipeline
* They are getting rejected without an interview
* They are seeing fake job postings and spam

## Objections & Blockers
People are stopped from solving these problems because:
* They don't know how to efficiently search for jobs
* They are not getting feedback from employers
* They are experiencing biased hiring practices
* They are using inefficient job search processes
* They are not using AI tools effectively
* They are not prepared for interviews
* They are not getting hired due to lack of experience or skills

## Biggest Opportunity
The biggest opportunity is to create a **Job Search Platform** that solves the problem of "I am seeing many anecdotal experiences shared online on various platforms stating that it is difficult to find employment in tech." This pain point is the most frequent, most severe, and least served by existing tools. The platform could provide a comprehensive job search experience, including job posting verification, candidate screening, and hiring process management. The rough willingness to pay signal is high, indicating that job seekers and employers are willing to pay for a solution to this problem.
