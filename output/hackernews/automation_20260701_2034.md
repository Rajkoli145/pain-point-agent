## Patterns & Triggers
People are frustrated with automation when:
* They are trying to automate complex workflows and encounter difficulties.
* They are using inadequate AI automation tools.
* They are struggling to identify tasks that can be automated.
* They are working with spreadsheets and encountering limitations.
* They are trying to introduce automation into their processes, but are unsure of how to start.
* They are spending too much time on botsitting AI, leading to job dissatisfaction.

## Objections & Blockers
People are stopped from solving their automation problems because:
* They lack knowledge about how to automate workflows.
* They are unsure about which tools to use for automation.
* They are struggling to identify automatable tasks.
* They are limited by the capabilities of their current automation solutions.
* They are frustrated with the complexity of automation and the time it takes to set up.

## Biggest Opportunity
The biggest opportunity is to develop an **Automation Workflow Tool** that can simplify the automation process for businesses and individuals. This tool should have a user-friendly interface, integrate with various automation tools, and provide features such as workflow automation, task identification, and AI-powered automation. The willingness to pay for such a tool is high, as people are willing to pay for solutions that can increase their productivity and simplify their automation processes. The difficulty of building such a tool is medium, as it requires developing a user-friendly interface and integrating with various automation tools.
