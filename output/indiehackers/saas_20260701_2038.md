## Patterns & Triggers
People are struggling with building and maintaining their SaaS websites, getting feedback from users, and finding viable SaaS ideas. They are also having difficulty with marketing and distribution, technical difficulties, and pricing. These complaints often arise when they are trying to launch a new SaaS product, or when they are trying to scale their existing product. Many have tried various solutions, such as building multiple SaaS products, focusing on marketing, and trying to understand distribution, but these efforts have been unsuccessful.

## Objections & Blockers
Many indie hackers are blocked by their lack of understanding of distribution, marketing, and pricing. They have tried building multiple SaaS products, but have failed to get paying customers. Some have also tried to focus on marketing, but have found it to be ineffective. Others have struggled with technical difficulties and infrastructure, which has prevented them from launching or scaling their SaaS products. These blockers have led to frustration and a sense of desperation among indie hackers.

## Biggest Opportunity
The biggest opportunity is to create a **Customer Acquisition Platform** that helps SaaS founders and marketers acquire paying customers. This pain point is most frequent, most severe, and least served by existing tools. Many indie hackers have struggled with getting paying customers, and have tried various solutions, but have been unsuccessful. A platform that provides a comprehensive solution to customer acquisition could charge $500-1000 per month and have a high willingness to pay signal. As one indie hacker said, "SaaS is insanely competitive, and without solid marketing, even a great product will go unnoticed."
